Qualitative Research on Chinese Student Usage Pattern

Snapchat is a popular social networking application among the young adults across the world. It has a lot of unique and funny features such as disappearing messages, filters, and Bitmoji.

However, Snapchat is unavailable in China due to various reasons. How would the young adults in China perceive Snapchat and would it be accepted in China is remained unanswered. So we conducted a qualitative research on Chinese international students in America to find out their usage pattern an attitudes towards it.

1. Recruit Participants

We recruited 8 participants (3 female) who are Chinese international students at a Northeastern American university. 5 of them never used Snapchat before. Some of them are friends and some of them do not know about each other.

2. Getting Familiar

A meeting of introducing basic functions of Snapchat was held at the beginning of the study. Participants were allowed to use Snapchat freely for one week.

3. Focus Group

We held two focus groups according to different schedules for particpants. Both focus group was semi-structured with the same questions. We recorded video and audio of both focus group based on their consent.

4. Data Collection

We transcribed the recorded audio into documents and counted keywords and phrases for determining themes such as using disappearing message for privacy and using Bitmoji for fun.
