Qualitative Research

Attitude Towards An Unavailable Social App In China

Snapchat is a popular social networking application among the young adults across the world. It has a lot of unique and funny features such as disappearing messages, filters, and Bitmoji.

However, Snapchat is unavailable in China due to various reasons. How would the young adults in China perceive Snapchat and would it be accepted in China is remained unanswered.

So we conducted a qualitative research on Chinese international students in America to find out their usage pattern an attitudes towards it.

1. Recruit Participants

We recruited 8 (3 female) Chinese international students at a Northeastern American university.

5 of them never used Snapchat before. None of them is Snapchat regular user.

4 of them are mutual friends. Only 1 person do not know any of the rest.

2. Snapchat Introduction

We held an introduction meeting of basic functions on Snapchat after recruting participants. We introduced the functions of Snapchat but not suggesting any usage. They could use Snapchat in any way they want.

Participants were encouraged to add friends with others in the meeting. After one week, they were required to described their experiences of using Snapchat in a foucs group.

3. Focus Group

We held two focus groups according to different schedules of particpants. Video and audio are recorded based on their consent.

Both focus groups was semi-structured with the general questions about the experiences of using Snapchat and its specific functions such as disappearing message and Bitmoji.

4. Data Collection

We transcribed the recorded audio and video into documents. The transcripts provided deep data with rich context.

We coded the transcription by counting keywords and phrases for determining themes that mentioned in previous literature, including using disappearing message for privacy and using Bitmoji for fun. Interesting and insightful comments were also quoted.

Due to the lack of IRB (Institutional Review Board) approval, we are not allowed to publish data from human object. Thus, the results cannot be demonstrated on this site.

Quantitative Research

International Usage Pattern of Snapchat

The result from the last study differed from our expectation of Snapchat. Snapchat might not easily achieve success in China due to the apps with similar functions in the market. But we do not know if usage patterns and attitutes regarding Snapchat itself differ by nationality.

1. Questionnaire Structure

Most questions are 5-points Likert Scale. Some are multiple choice questions regarding specific features on Snapchat.

  1. Demographic information
  2. Attitudes towards specific features on Snapchat, for example, disappearing messages.
  3. Usability measurements from Lund's USE questionnaire
  4. Intensity and Social Bridging questionnaire from Piwek and Joinson.

It takes around 10 minutes to complete the questionnaire.

2. Spread out questionnaries

We spread the questionnaire to people by the Internet to countries in Europe, America, and Asia.
