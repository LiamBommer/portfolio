A Complement for WeChat Teamwork

WeChat has been the most popular social app in China for years. Recently, WeChat launched new version with WeChat Mini-program that provides similar experiences as native apps without leaving the WeChat interface, enhancing user convenience.

WeChat is an popular instant messenger application for daily communication, but it caused a few troubles for teamworking such as arranging activities. With the help of Mini-program, WeChat is endowed with countless possibilities. One Cooperation is built to provide better teamwork support in WeChat.

User Interview

Teams that communicate and cooperate on WeChat are our main users, for example, college students. Aiming to figure out their problems in team cooperation and find a better way to simple, flexible and efficient tasks managing, we invited 12 college students with rich experiences in arranging social activities and team competition.

In order to obtain well-rounded information, we recorded every interview and transcribed into documents. Then gathered all interview documents to do synthesis and find insights. Below are the four pain points and solution we came out with:

"Hard to clearly demonstrate distrubuted work"

Task

Clear distribution on task boards
"Announcements may often be missed"

Announcement

Visualizied if everyone checked the announcement
"Timeline is unclear in the chat history"

Schedule

Clear timeline and milestones
"Members are afraid of expressing ideas"

Idea Wall

Anonymous brainstorming space for expressing ideas

Questionnaire

Given our available resources, we published questionnaires to verify our assumption and filtering potential features.

According to the this question, 91.7% of students use WeChat for team cooperation.

Less than 10% of students have ever used team cooperation apps.

According to the question asking if they have to be careful about their tone when chatting with their colleagues, up to 70% of students agree when making comments or suggestions.

According to the results of more than 200 questionnaires from more than 7 provinces in China, we were able to make sure that many college students have encountered the problems we found out in the user interviews.

Product Structure

Refer to the user interviews and questionnaires we have done, we compiled the planning of all functions and modules.

Prototyping

In this project, we used Axure RP as our prototyping tool. In order to reduce the learning cost, we adopted the visual specifications and UI components that are similar to WeChat.

Announcement

  • No more announcing by "@everyone"
  • Click 'Got it' (green button) to show you've read the announcement
  • Clear to see who has read the announcement

Schedule

  • Visible timeline and milestone
  • Milestone that is connected to a task

Idea Wall

  • An anonymous space for members to voice their ideas
  • No more embarrasment

Technical Realization

I was the front-end developer in this project. WeChat Mini-program uses programming language WXML, WXSS which are similar to HTML and CSS. Also, a Model-View-ViewModel javascript framework similar to Vue.js is used in WeChat.

Particularly, I’ve practiced some CSS3 animation in the project.

Design Iteration

After receiving suggestions from users and as we kept using the app ourselves, we were continuously improving the Mini-program.

Project List

The index is almost the most important page of the whole app. However, this design shows too little information to users. So we decided to provide more useful information about the project on the index page, such as the latest tasks, schedules, and meetings

The picture in the middle is the sketch and in the right is the actual page we made. In addition to the previous information, we put the latest meetings and tasks in the second and third line. The tasks with a deadline of only one day appear red. Also, users can click the arrow to fold the details.

Task Detail

This is a page showing details of a specific task, including task name, task description, members, deadline, subtasks, and comments. In the original design, every detail and operation lies as a list item occupies a line. It could cause confusion, such as the red text ‘delete task’ just lying there.

After redesigning, we arranged different kinds of details, sorted out different information modules, and put all operation into the ellipsis icon in the upper right corner. Each information modules would be hidden or folded if it is not used.
