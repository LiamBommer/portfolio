What is Jiao Xiang

Jiao Xiang ('交想') means 'Co-Think', it is a moblie app that enable us to read papers and share ideas with other readers in a similar way with Medium. It combines socialized and gamified elements into traditional reading experiences.

Nowadays, people spend hours on swiping contents on their phones everyday. The reading experience is shallow and fragmented. Applications like Forest and Medium have proven that involving socialized and gamified elements can raise people's interest in the app. Jiao Xiang was designed to utilize those elements to improve mobile reading experience.

User Interview

Young adults are majority population who consume entertainment and spend the most time on smartphones. I interviewed 8 college students at a south China university to investigate the following usage of smartphones and learned that:

1. At what timing people would browse their phones?

Mostly in a relatively short period of time (<20mins) such as during classes, waiting in lines, having a meal, and before bed.

2. What type of content would they usually browse?

  • Entertaining information such as short videos and gossips
  • Short articles for knowledge on various platforms

3. What drives them to browse the phone?

  1. When distracted or bored from the things they are doing
  2. Complete repeated activitites such as daily tasks in games
  3. Got stimulated by competitive or intriguing content

Competitive Analysis

Medium is a popular reading and writing platform on the Internet. Social elements play an important role in this community.

Users can highlight, comment, and share certain sentences in an article (see figure on the right). You will read the highlights and comments from your following in the same article. In the profile page, users can see the article they read and comments they made.

WeChat Reading is one of the most popular reading app in the Chinese market. Users have access with various books and novels in the app.

Similar to Medium, WeChat Reading allow users to highlight and comments sentences in the book which can be see by other users. Beside that, WeChat Reading calculates the total time users spend on reading every week and generate friends rank (see figure on the right) based on your friends list in WeChat.

Prototyping with Balsamiq

Key Functions

Shared Highlight

In an article, sentences marked by other users appear teal. Users can highlight with a click of the sentence. Then the sentence turned blue and relative actions popup such as comments and undo.

After important parts being highlighted by other users, the article becomes easier to read. This function actively connects users when reading.

Thoughts

Users can comment on the highlighted sentences and share their ideas about it.

Thoughts from other users may provide more context and information for the article.

Interest Radar

Articles are categorized into different interest such as science, technology, art, etc. Highlighting and commenting on articles from different categories accumulates the points in this interest.

By reading articles from different categories, users' interest and characteristics will be reflected by the interest radar chart.

Reading History

The highlights and comments made by the user will be shown in his profile page.
