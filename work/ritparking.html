Hard to find parking spots

In RIT, a lot of students and professors drive to the campus. It is hard to find parking spots during the class time on weekdays. My teammates and I usually spend at least 5 to 10 minutes finding parking spots. To obtain a better understanding of the parking problmes in RIT, we conducted contextual interviews.

Contextual Interviews

First, we interviewed one of our teammates about the parking problems that she encountered. The problems that we experienced contributed to the semi-structured interviews. We went to the parking lots to observed how people parked and found parking spots. We then interviewed 2 people in the parking lot and 7 people on the campus.

After segmenting interview data into notes, we synthesised them into different hierarchies and built an affinity diagram.

Previous Next

By analysizing and synthesising interview data, we concluded three main problems that users might encounter when parking in RIT:

Is there any vacant spot?

Vacant spots are hard to find during peak hours in parking lots that are close to buildings. People usually spend a lot of time circling around and search for spots.

Where is my car?

Some people may forget where their car is in the large parking lots in RIT, especially after spending the whole day on campus.

What are the parking rules?

RIT has different parking rules for different permits. People new to RIT may forget their rules.

Lo-fi Prototype

We first drafted our prototype on a whiteboard. Then, we brought our ideas on paper and tested basic functions with people by switching the paper. In the early testing, we found out flaws in the process and kept iterating our design.

Hi-fi Prototype

When sign up, users can seletec their permit type. The parking rules are visualized into the process of finding spots according to user's permit type. There are two main task flows for primary users: find a vacant spot before coming to the campus and find their cars.

Sign up and specify permit type

Users can specify their permit type (Genernal or Reserved). Available parking spots on display will be adjusted according to their permit type.

Find vacant spots

Users search or move the map to select destination. Parking lots near the destination will be suggested. Users navigate to the parking lot by navigation apps.
